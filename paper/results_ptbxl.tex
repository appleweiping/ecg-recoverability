\subsection{Geometry, not lead count}
On the PTB-XL QRS dipolar subspace, $\kappa_s(S)$ ranks configurations by
\emph{conditioning}: a single Lead~I observes one of three dipole directions
(effective rank $1$); a spread triplet $\{$I,\,II,\,V2$\}$ gives $\kappa{=}\kapSpan$
(rank $3$); three adjacent precordials $\{$V1,\,V2,\,V3$\}$ give $\kappa{=}\kapColl$;
but three \emph{coplanar} limb leads $\{$I,\,II,\,III$\}$ and the six limb leads have
$\kappa$ of order $10^{5}$ and $10^{4}$ and \emph{effective rank $2$}---their third
(transverse) dipole direction is Tier~III. This is a property of the lead set alone,
available before any reconstruction: it says, upfront, that limb leads cannot recover
the transverse-dipole content that precordial ST depends on.

\subsection{The certificate localises and explains}
Table~\ref{tab:hall} reconstructs the unobserved leads from $\{$I,\,II,\,V2$\}$ with
three methods. A scalar RMSE ranks them (the generative model is worst) but does not
say \emph{which} feature is wrong or \emph{why}. The certificate does. The dipolar
baseline places \emph{zero} energy in the certified-unrecoverable subspace ($h{=}0$):
it never fabricates, but recovers only Tier~I. The learned linear (OLS) reconstructor
places moderate energy there whose oracle correlation $\rho$ with the true non-dipolar
content is \emph{positive}---it genuinely recovers Tier~II. The generative
reconstructor places more energy there, uncorrelated with truth ($\rho\!\approx\!0$).
We stress the honest scope: $\rho$ is an oracle validation, and $h$ alone need not
separate blur from fabrication when energies match---the deployable guarantee is the
configuration-level certificate ($\kappa$, tiers) plus the held-out flag.
