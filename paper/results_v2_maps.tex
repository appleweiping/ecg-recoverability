\subsection{The recoverability map on PTB-XL}
We estimate $\bm M_s$ (rank 3) per segment on the training NORM records and report the map with
RECORD-LEVEL bootstrap CIs (each resample draws whole records, re-pools their segment samples,
and refits $\bm M_s$; truncation tolerance $\varrho{=}10^{-2}$; Fig.~\ref{fig:map}). The map is
a deterministic function of the estimated $\bm M_s$ and the selection $S$; the CI quantifies its
estimation uncertainty. A single observed lead leaves every other lead's dipolar component
unidentifiable; a dipole-spanning triplet $\{$I,II,V2$\}$ or the five-lead $\{$I,II,V1,V3,V5$\}$
makes all targets \emph{exactly} identifiable ($\eta{=}0$ to machine precision, since $S$ spans
the rank-3 subspace).

\textbf{Exact vs.\ graded identifiability.} Exact identifiability is the binary test
$\eta_{s,\ell}(S){=}0$. For limb-6, \emph{no} precordial lead is exactly identifiable
($\eta_{\mathrm{ST},\ell}{>}0$ for all), so we grade the shortfall with two scale-aware
quantities rather than raw $\eta$: the \emph{normalized} identifiability
$\tilde\eta_{s,\ell}=\eta_{s,\ell}/\|\bm e_\ell^\top\bm M_s\|_2\in[0,1]$ (the fraction of the
lead's dipolar content that is unobservable) and the \emph{expected unobserved ambiguity} in mV
under the fitted dipole-coordinate prior. Both fall from strongly unrecoverable anterior leads
(V2 $\tilde\eta{=}\EtaNormVtwo$, ambiguity $\AmbVtwo$\,mV; V3, V1, V4) to less-ambiguous lateral
leads (V5 $\tilde\eta{=}\EtaNormVfive$, $\AmbVfive$\,mV; V6 $\tilde\eta{=}\EtaNormVsix$,
$\AmbVsix$\,mV). We therefore say \textbf{anterior precordial ST (V1--V4) is far from
identifiable from limb leads, while lateral V5/V6 are much less ambiguous---but none is exactly
identifiable}. A physiological precordial/limb split cannot make this graded call; the empirical
map does.

\textbf{Downstream ST cost across reconstructors.} We reconstruct the full 10-second waveform
with three real continuous linear reconstructors (observed leads kept exact; ST measured at
J$+$60\,ms vs.\ a PR baseline on fiducials located once on the observed Lead~II and shared
between truth and reconstruction), and count ST-threshold events ($|$ST$|{\ge}0.1$\,mV, elevation
or depression; false positive = crossing in reconstruction not truth, false negative = vice
versa; Table~\ref{tab:safety}). The \emph{total} wrong-event rate is \emph{empirically similar}
across the evaluated reconstructors ($\TotWrongLo$--$\TotWrongHi\%$); all three \emph{miss} more
true crossings than they invent (FN$>$FP)---limb$\to$precordial reconstruction smooths ST toward
the population and drops real deviations---and the FP/FN balance shifts with the reconstructor
(the dipolar recovery has the largest false-positive share, OLS the smallest). We report the
whole matrix; because we do not derive a minimax/Bayes lower bound, we
describe the shared total as an empirically similar total error rate across these three
reconstructors---\emph{not} a certified floor.

\textbf{Truncation-tolerance sensitivity.} Rank/$\kappa$ of well-posed configurations are
stable across $\varrho\!\in\!\{10^{-4},\dots,10^{-1}\}$ (e.g.\ $\{$I,II,V1,V3,V5$\}$ is
rank~3 throughout), while near-rank-deficient sets are sensitive: on synthetic data
$\{$V1,V2,V3$\}$ is rank~2 at $\varrho{=}10^{-2}$ but rank~3 with $\kappa{\sim}200$ at
$\varrho{=}10^{-4}$. We therefore report a single $\kappa$ only for well-conditioned
configurations and give the $\varrho$-sweep and bootstrap CIs otherwise; a single $10^{4}$
or $10^{5}$ figure for a degenerate set is not meaningful.
