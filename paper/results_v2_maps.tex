\subsection{The recoverability map on PTB-XL}
We estimate $\bm M_s$ (rank 3) per segment on the training folds and evaluate the map on
$1{,}200$ NORM records (per-lead $\eta,\kappa$ with $200$-sample bootstrap CIs, truncation
tolerance $\varrho{=}10^{-2}$; Fig.~\ref{fig:map}). The map is per target lead: a single
observed lead (e.g.\ Lead~I) leaves \emph{every} other lead's dipolar component
unidentifiable ($\eta{>}0$; rank~1); a dipole-spanning triplet $\{$I,II,V2$\}$ or the
five-lead $\{$I,II,V1,V3,V5$\}$ makes all targets identifiable ($\eta{\approx}0$). For the
six limb leads, \emph{all} precordial leads V1--V6 are unidentifiable for QRS/ST/T: the
transverse dipole is unobserved, so precordial ST cannot be read from limb leads---a
warning available before any reconstruction.

\textbf{Certificate-level safety.} The warning is actionable. On $1{,}498$ delineated
fold-10 records, reconstructing precordial ST from limb-6 with a real dipolar reconstructor
produces $32.5\%$ fabricated (CI $[29.9,34.9]$) and $17.6\%$ masked (CI $[15.8,19.7]$)
ST-threshold events (a $|$ST$|{>}0.1$\,mV crossing present in the reconstruction but not the
truth, or vice-versa), at a mean ST error of $0.076$\,mV---the cost of the certified
unidentifiability ($\eta_{\mathrm{ST},\ell}{>}0$ for every precordial $\ell$). We report ST-
threshold events, not diagnoses.

\textbf{Truncation-tolerance sensitivity.} Rank/$\kappa$ of well-posed configurations are
stable across $\varrho\!\in\!\{10^{-4},\dots,10^{-1}\}$ (e.g.\ $\{$I,II,V1,V3,V5$\}$ is
rank~3 throughout), while near-rank-deficient sets are sensitive: on synthetic data
$\{$V1,V2,V3$\}$ is rank~2 at $\varrho{=}10^{-2}$ but rank~3 with $\kappa{\sim}200$ at
$\varrho{=}10^{-4}$. We therefore report a single $\kappa$ only for well-conditioned
configurations and give the $\varrho$-sweep and bootstrap CIs otherwise; a single $10^{4}$
or $10^{5}$ figure for a degenerate set is not meaningful.
