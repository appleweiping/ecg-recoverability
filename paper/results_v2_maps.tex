\subsection{The recoverability map on PTB-XL}
We estimate $\bm M_s$ (rank 3) per segment on the training folds and evaluate the map on
$1{,}200$ NORM records disjoint from the estimation folds (per-lead $\eta,\kappa$ with
$200$-sample bootstrap CIs, truncation tolerance $\varrho{=}10^{-2}$; Fig.~\ref{fig:map}).
The map is \emph{graded and per target lead}: a single observed lead (e.g.\ Lead~I) leaves
every other lead's dipolar component unidentifiable ($\eta{>}0$; rank~1); a dipole-spanning
triplet $\{$I,II,V2$\}$ or the five-lead $\{$I,II,V1,V3,V5$\}$ makes all targets identifiable
($\eta{\approx}0$ to machine precision, since $S$ then spans the rank-3 subspace). The value
over a binary rank test is the \emph{gradient} it resolves: for limb-6 the ST identifiability
falls smoothly from strongly unidentifiable anterior leads
(V2\,$\eta{=}\EtaSTVtwo$, V3\,$\EtaSTVthree$, V1\,$\EtaSTVone$, V4\,$\EtaSTVfour$) to
\emph{near-identifiable} lateral leads (V5\,$\EtaSTVfive$, V6\,$\EtaSTVsix$). So the honest a
priori warning is specific: \textbf{anterior precordial ST (V1--V4) is not identifiable from
limb leads}---the direction of the estimated subspace it needs is unobserved---while lateral
V5/V6 are largely recoverable. A physiological binary ``precordial vs.\ limb'' split cannot
make this call; the empirical graded map does.

\textbf{Certificate-level safety is reconstructor-invariant at the floor.} The warning is
actionable but must be stated on a reconstructor-\emph{invariant} quantity. On $\SafetyN$
delineated fold-10 records we reconstruct anterior precordial ST from limb-6 with three
reconstructors and count ST-threshold events ($|$ST$|{>}0.1$\,mV crossings)---false positives
(a crossing in the reconstruction, not the truth) and false negatives (vice versa;
Table~\ref{tab:safety}). The \emph{total} wrong-event rate is near-invariant across
reconstructors ($\TotWrongLo$--$\TotWrongHi\%$, at mean ST error $\ErrRidge$--$\ErrOls$\,mV):
this floor is the cost of the certified unidentifiability. What the reconstructor \emph{does}
choose is the false-positive/false-negative split---dipolar leans to false positives
($\FabDipolar\%$/$\MskDipolar\%$), OLS to false negatives ($\FabOls\%$/$\MskOls\%$)---a design
trade-off, not a certified number. We therefore report the whole matrix and anchor the
certificate on the invariant floor, not a single cell.

\textbf{Truncation-tolerance sensitivity.} Rank/$\kappa$ of well-posed configurations are
stable across $\varrho\!\in\!\{10^{-4},\dots,10^{-1}\}$ (e.g.\ $\{$I,II,V1,V3,V5$\}$ is
rank~3 throughout), while near-rank-deficient sets are sensitive: on synthetic data
$\{$V1,V2,V3$\}$ is rank~2 at $\varrho{=}10^{-2}$ but rank~3 with $\kappa{\sim}200$ at
$\varrho{=}10^{-4}$. We therefore report a single $\kappa$ only for well-conditioned
configurations and give the $\varrho$-sweep and bootstrap CIs otherwise; a single $10^{4}$
or $10^{5}$ figure for a degenerate set is not meaningful.
