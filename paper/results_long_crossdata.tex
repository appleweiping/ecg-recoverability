\section{Cross-Dataset Transfer of the Certificate}
\label{sec:cross}
The certificate is a function of the estimated subspace $\bm M_s$ and the selection $S$; a
fair question is whether it is an artifact of one cohort. We re-estimate $\bm M_s$ and the
per-configuration conditioning on the independent Chapman-Shaoxing 12-lead database
\cite{zheng2020chapman} (different country, devices, and sampling) and compare to PTB-XL
(\texttt{results/cross\_dataset.json}); the re-estimation uses $349$ Chapman records
($\sim$13.8k segments per segment type). We deliberately do \emph{not} claim ``population
independence''; we report what actually transfers.

\textbf{QRS subspace is transfer-stable.} For QRS the two datasets' rank-3 subspaces are nearly
aligned (principal angles $20.3^\circ/9.1^\circ/1.6^\circ$; explained-variance ratios
$0.56/0.27/0.08$ vs.\ $0.55/0.27/0.06$). The per-configuration conditioning transfers for the well-posed sets: $\kappa$
for $\{$I,II,V2$\}$ is $3.1$ (PTB-XL) vs.\ $4.1$ (Chapman) and for $\{$I,II,V1,V3,V5$\}$ $2.5$
vs.\ $2.3$, and the limb-6 ill-conditioning is reproduced on both
($\kappa\!\sim\!2\times10^{5}$). The near-rank-deficient $\{$V1,V2,V3$\}$ transfers less
cleanly ($3.9$ vs.\ $6.7$, a $\sim$70\% increase), as expected for an ill-conditioned set.
So the qualitative identifiable/unidentifiable verdict and the $\kappa$ ordering are stable for
well-posed configurations, with larger cohort variation on the near-degenerate ones.

\textbf{ST/T third direction does not transfer.} For the low-amplitude segments (P, ST, T)
two of three subspace directions align but the third differs sharply between datasets
(max principal angle $88$--$89^\circ$; P $88.4^\circ$, ST $88.3^\circ$, T $89.2^\circ$). We therefore restrict the transfer claim: the QRS map
and the limb-6 conditioning verdict are cohort-stable; the ST/T \emph{third} spatial direction
is cohort-specific and its map should be re-estimated per dataset. This is the honest scope of
transfer, not a blanket invariance.

\textbf{Sensitivity to duplicated limb leads (\texttt{results/lead\_weighting.json}).} The six
limb leads are exact combinations of I and II, so a 12-lead PCA up-weights frontal-plane content.
Refitting $\bm M_s$ on the 8 algebraically-independent leads and lifting back via the fixed
transform $\bm T$, the \emph{identifiability verdict is robust}: for the spanning sets the
per-lead $\eta$ is unchanged (max $|\Delta\eta|{=}0$; all targets remain exactly identifiable),
and limb-6 precordial leads stay unidentifiable under both fits. The \emph{magnitudes} of the
low-amplitude ST/T subspace are, however, sensitive: the 12-lead and 8-lead ST/T subspaces
differ by large principal angles ($75^\circ$/$82^\circ$) and $\kappa$ can shift several-fold
(e.g.\ ST $\{$I,II,V2$\}$: $3.8\!\to\!38$). We therefore read the QRS map quantitatively and the
ST/T \emph{graded} values qualitatively---the binary identifiable/unidentifiable verdict is
robust; the exact ST/T $\eta$/$\kappa$/ambiguity magnitudes are weighting-dependent.
