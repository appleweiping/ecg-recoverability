\subsection*{Cross-cohort sensitivity of the verdict}
\label{sec:transfer}
The certificate is computed from a \emph{fitted} dipolar subspace $\bm M_s$, so it is natural to ask
how the verdict moves across cohorts. One fact is exact and useful:
$\eta_{s,\ell}(S)=\|\bm e_\ell^\top\bm M_s(\bm I-\bm P_{\mathrm{obs}})\|_2$ depends on $\bm M_s$
\emph{only through its column space}. Replacing $\bm M_s$ by $\bm M_s\bm R$ for any $\bm R\in O(3)$
rotates $\bm P_{\mathrm{obs}}$ and $\bm e_\ell^\top\bm M_s$ together and leaves every $\eta$ unchanged;
hence two cohorts that share a dipolar subspace (all principal angles zero) have identical verdicts,
and in general the drift is governed by how far the two subspaces separate.

A $\sin\Theta$ (Davis--Kahan~\cite{davis1970rotation}) perturbation makes this quantitative but, at
the conditioning we observe, \emph{vacuously so}. After optimal alignment,
$|\eta^A_{s,\ell}(S)-\eta^B_{s,\ell}(S)|\le 2\sin(\theta^\star/2)\,(1+2\,\kappa_s)$, where
$\theta^\star$ is the largest principal angle between the two cohorts' segment-$s$ dipolar subspaces
and $\kappa_s=\max(\kappa_s^A,\kappa_s^B)$ is the larger observed-set conditioning; the
projector-difference step uses the Stewart--Wedin bound and assumes the observed block keeps its rank
across cohorts and that the $\varrho$-truncated projector coincides with the exact one. On
PTB-XL$\to$Chapman (limb-6$\to$V2) the right-hand side evaluates to $\TransferRhsQRS$ (QRS) and
$\TransferRhsST$ (ST), \emph{both exceeding} the trivial bound $|\Delta\eta|\le1$ (since
$\eta\in[0,1]$). The inequality therefore \textbf{certifies nothing here}: it does not establish that
the QRS verdict transfers, and it is not a pre-deployment guarantee. A non-vacuous statement would
need a target-specific bound built from the aligned target-lead rows and the exact projector
difference; we leave that to future work and do not claim it.

We accordingly present the cross-cohort comparison as \emph{empirical sensitivity}, not a certified
bound (\texttt{results/transfer\_bound.json}). The measured principal angle and the measured verdict
drift agree qualitatively: for QRS the two dipolar subspaces are nearly aligned
($\theta^\star{=}\TransferAngleQRS^\circ$; $\eta$ drift $\TransferDriftQRS$), whereas for ST they are
nearly orthogonal ($\theta^\star{=}\TransferAngleST^\circ$, CI up to $89.6^\circ$) with a larger but
highly uncertain drift (the Chapman $\eta$ bootstrap CI is $[0.02,0.71]$). This is a two-cohort
observation---the QRS dipolar geometry happens to be shared between these cohorts while the
low-amplitude ST geometry is not, consistent with the subspace-angle ablations of
Sec.~\ref{sec:limits}---not a guarantee about unseen cohorts.
